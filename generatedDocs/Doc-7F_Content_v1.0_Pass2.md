## §4 — Routing & Invitation Observability *(mechanism only; Doc-5E §7; engine out-of-wire)*

### §4.1 Observability reads only
The buyer **observes** routing/invitation outcomes via the verified caller-leg reads: **`get_routing_log`**, **`get_invitation`**, **`list_invitations`** (Doc-5E §7). **`get_matching_results` is NOT bound** (Admin leg + internal-service — anti-gaming; R5; FR3). The buyer sees **which vendors were invited and why** (explainable routing log) — never the raw matching scores/ranking.

### §4.2 The engine is out-of-wire; the buyer never invokes it
The matching/routing engine (`run_matching_pipeline`, `assemble_and_route_wave`, `replenish_wave`, `drain_deferred_queue`, etc.) is **System out-of-wire** (Doc-5E §8). The buyer **never invokes matching/routing** and **never sends an invitation** (Pass-1 §2; the engine generates invitations per governed routing). The UI **displays** routing state; it never drives it.

### §4.3 Non-disclosing explainability (R5)
The routing-log / invitation reads render **explainability without exposing a protected fact** (`Doc-5E R5`): no other tenant's data, no protected vendor signal, no excluded-vendor inference. **No payment/entitlement value influences the routing display** (R7) — the buyer never sees a vendor included/excluded by payment.

## §5 — Quotation Viewing *(mechanism only; Doc-5E §5; visibility-gated)*

### §5.1 Binding
Bind **`get_quotation`** / **`list_quotations_for_rfq`** (Doc-5E §5, Buyer leg) — **`quotation_visibility`-gated**. The buyer views quotations submitted to its RFQ; a quotation outside the buyer's visibility scope collapses to **`NOT_FOUND`** (`Doc-5A §6.3`).

### §5.2 Read-only; vendor authoring is Doc-7G
The buyer **reads** quotations; **vendor quotation authoring** (`submit_quotation`/`revise_quotation`/`withdraw_quotation` — Doc-5E §5 Vendor leg) is **Doc-7G**, not here. The buyer never edits a vendor's quotation.

## §6 — Comparison & Award *(mechanism only; Doc-5E §6; R6 — the no-auto-decision moat)*

### §6.1 Comparison statement is read-only decision support
Bind **`get_comparison_statement`** (Buyer read) — a **read-only decision-support** view **generated by the System engine** (`generate_comparison_statement`, §8 out-of-wire). The buyer **reads** the comparison; it **never generates** it, and the comparison is **decision support, not a recommendation**.

### §6.2 Evaluation & award commands
Bind **`shortlist_quotation`**, **`manage_clarification`** (+ the M6 RFQ/clarification thread — `Doc-5H`, composed, non-disclosure-bound), **`invoke_best_and_final`**, **`award_rfq`** (explicit Buyer command), **`close_lost_rfq`** (Doc-5E §6).

### §6.3 No auto-decision (R6) — the load-bearing moat
**The UI — and the AI advisory panel — NEVER auto-recommend, auto-rank-to-winner, auto-select, or present a "recommended winner"** (`Doc-5E R6`; `Doc-3 §9.1` FIXED; Invariant #12). The comparison view presents quotations side-by-side as **decision support**; **`award_rfq` is a deliberate, unranked buyer choice**. The AI advisory panel (Pass-3 §10) may summarize/draft/explain but **never ranks-to-winner or recommends a vendor**. No surface gates or pre-selects the award.

### §6.4 State machine
Shortlist/clarification/best-and-final/award/close render only Doc-4M-permitted Buyer transitions (`Doc-7A §7`); STATE/CONFLICT (409) → reconcile. `award_rfq` drives the RFQ to `closed_won` (Doc-4M); `close_lost_rfq` to `closed_lost`.

## §7 — Post-Award Operations *(mechanism only; Doc-5F buyer-leg; R7 seam; R8 money-boundary)*

### §7.1 The post-award seam (R7) — engagement created by System on award
The **engagement is created by the System** consumer of `RFQClosedWon` (`create_engagement_on_award`, §9 out-of-wire — `Doc-5F R7`); the **buyer does not create it**. The buyer **manages the engagement after it exists**. Doc-7F binds the **buyer-leg management** commands, never `create_engagement` (System).

### §7.2 Engagement & commercial documents (BC-OPS-2 buyer-leg)
Bind the buyer-leg of: **`update_engagement_status`**, **`close_engagement`**, **`record_delivery`**, **`record_buyer_feedback`**, **`issue_engagement_document`** (LOI/PO/WCC the buyer issues), **`revise_engagement_document`**, **`issue_trade_invoice`** / **`update_trade_invoice_status`**, **`record_payment`** / **`confirm_payment`** + reads (`get_engagement`, `list_engagements`, `get_engagement_document`). BC-OPS-2 is **two-sided (buyer/vendor)** — Doc-7F binds the **buyer leg** (issues/approves); the **vendor leg is Doc-7G**.

### §7.3 Money-boundary (R8) — records, not settlement
**The platform handles no buyer↔vendor money** (`Doc-5F R8`): `record_payment`/`confirm_payment` and `issue_trade_invoice` create **records / workflow state**, **never settlement, escrow, custody, or fund movement**. A **trade invoice is an operations record (≠ the platform billing invoice** — `Doc-5I R6`; the latter is the Account shell, Doc-7E). The buyer records that money moved (outside the platform); the platform never moves it.

### §7.4 Templates & generated documents (BC-OPS-4 buyer-leg)
Bind the buyer-leg template management (`create_template`/`add_template_version`/`activate_template`/`archive_template`/`reactivate_template`) + generated-document grants (`grant_generated_document`/`revoke_generated_document_grant`) + reads. **`generate_document` is a System async job** (§9 out-of-wire — `Doc-5F R9`) — the UI **requests/reads** generated documents (via the grant model), never runs generation client-side.

### §7.5 Finance records (BC-OPS-5 buyer-leg)
Bind `create_finance_record`/`update_finance_record` + reads (BC-OPS-5, buyer leg). No lifecycle machine; currency-per-field default BDT (`Doc-2 §0.4`).
