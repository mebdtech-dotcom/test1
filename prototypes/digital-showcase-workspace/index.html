The governing idea

Content ≠ Presentation

Your profile, products, specs and projects are one content model. A template changes layout, hierarchy, density and visual grammar — never what the content is, who owns it, or who may see it. Switching templates re-presents the same published material; it never rewrites, republishes, or hides anything you didn't hide yourself.

Your business type never locks you to a template. It may suggest which sections to emphasize — you always choose the template, and nothing is switched for you.

Choose your template and arrange your sections

Two independent choices on one page. The template sets the visual style; the section arrangement sets what your public site leads with. Changing either never changes the other, and neither changes your content.

Arrange your sections

This section is the hybrid ruling's mechanism. Section emphasis lives in profile_sections (display_order + is_visible FROZEN) — not in template identity. That is why a business shape can be emphasized without minting a template per business type or locking a vendor to one. The starting points below are documented arrangements (the audited reference set): applying one reorders sections only and never changes your template. The converse holds too — choosing a template above never touches your section order or visibility. That two-way independence is the proof that arrangement ⟂ template.
